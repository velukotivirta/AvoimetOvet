Odin's lasagna



Lasagna for the ages.

This lasagna recipe has been given from parent to child in the Montereggioni family for the last 5 generations.

Now it's delicious, wavy, sauceful zest is here for you to make as well.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 pound ground beef chuck
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 1/2 large paprika, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can good-quality tomato sauce
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 3 ounces tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh oregano, or 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (preferably flat leaf)
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 pinch garlic powder and/or garlic salt
  • 1 tablespoon red or white wine vinegar
  • Salt to taste
Assembly of lasagna
  • 1/2 pound dry lasagna noodles
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 pounds mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 pound freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Method

  1. Put pasta water on to boil

    2. Put a large pot of salted water on the stovetop on high heat.

    It can take a while for a large pot of water to come to a boil,

    so prepare the sauce in the next steps while the water is heating.

  2. Brown the ground beef

    3. In a large skillet heat 2 teaspoons of olive oil on medium-high heat.

    Add the ground beef and cook until it is lightly browned on all sides.

    Remove the beef with a slotted spoon to a bowl. Drain off all but a tablespoon of fat.

  3. Cook the bell pepper, onions, and garlic; add back the beef

    4. Add the diced bell pepper and onions to the skillet.

    Cook for 4 to 5 minutes, until the onions are translucent and the peppers softened.

    Add the minced garlic and cook half a minute more.

    Return the browned ground beef to the pan.

    Stir to combine, reduce the heat to low, and cook for another 5 minutes.

  4. Make the sauce

    5. Transfer the beef mixture to a medium-sized pot.

    Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste to the pot.

    Add seasonings, sprinkle with red or white wine vinegar.

    Bring the sauce to a simmer and then lower the heat to maintain a low simmer.

    Cook for 15 to 45 minutes, stirring often. Scrape the bottom of the pot every so often so nothing sticks to the bottom and scorches.

  5. Boil and drain the lasagna

    6. Stir often to prevent from sticking.

  6. Preheat the oven to 200 celcius

  7. Assemble the lasagna

    8. Ladle a cup of sauce and spread it over the bottom of the dish.

    Arrange one layer of lasagna noodles lengthwise over the sauce.

    Ladle a third of the remaining sauce over the noodles.

    Sprinkle a layer of a third of the grated mozzarella on top of the lasagna sauce.

    Add half of the ricotta cheese, by placing cheese dollops every couple of inches.

    Sprinkle half the grated parmesan cheese evenly over the top of the ricotta cheese.

    Apply the second layer of noodles and top it with half of the remaining sauce.

    Add half of the remaining mozzarella, the remaining ricotta cheese, and another the remaining Parmesan.

    Finish with another layer of noodles.

    Spread the remaining sauce over the top layer of noodles and sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella cheese.

  8. Bake

    9. Bake at 200 for 45 minutes. Uncover the foil-covered lasagna in the last 10 minutes if you'd like more of a crusty top or edges.

  9. Cool and serve!
